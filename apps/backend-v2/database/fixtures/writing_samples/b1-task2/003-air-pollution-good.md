Air pollution is becoming a serious problem in many cities around the world. Some people believe that only the government should solve this problem. However, I think both government and individuals have important roles to play.

First, the government can make strict laws to control pollution from factories and vehicles. For example, they can limit cars in the city center and encourage people to use public transport like buses and trains. The government can also invest in clean energy like solar power and wind power to reduce the use of coal and oil. In addition, the government should educate people about the dangers of air pollution through schools and media campaigns.

Second, individuals also have responsibility. People can do small things like using public transport instead of driving a car. This will reduce the smoke from vehicles. They can also plant trees around their houses and in their neighborhood because trees help clean the air and make it fresh. Furthermore, people can save electricity at home by turning off lights and appliances when they are not using them. This will reduce the pollution from power plants that burn coal to make electricity. If everyone does a little bit every day, it can make a big difference for the environment.

In conclusion, I believe that solving air pollution need both government and individuals to work together. Only when both sides take action can we have clean air and a healthy environment for future generations.
